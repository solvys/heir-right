Florida Statute 732.401  
Florida Statute 732.401 — “Descent of Homestead” — is a Florida law that governs how a 
homestead property is inherited when the owner dies without a permitted devise (i.e., 
leaving a valid will or transfer) and is survived by a spouse and/or descendants.   
✅ What the statute says 
1.​ If the homestead is not devised (by will or other allowed means): 
○​ The homestead property descends like other intestate property (property of 
someone who died without a specific devise).   
○​ But if the decedent is survived by both a spouse and one or more 
descendants (children, grandchildren, etc.), then the surviving spouse gets 
a life estate in the homestead, and the descendants get a vested remainder 
interest, per stirpes (i.e., by branch of descent).   
2.​ Alternative election by surviving spouse: 
○​ Instead of taking the life estate, the surviving spouse may elect (choose) to 
take an undivided one-half interest in the homestead as a tenant in 
common, with the remaining half vesting in the descendants per stirpes.   
○​ This election must occur within 6 months after the decedent’s death and 
during the surviving spouse’s lifetime.   
○​ The election is irrevocable once made.   
○​ Notice of the election must be filed (recorded) with a legal description of 
the property in the county’s official records.   
3.​ Expenses of ownership 
○​ While the life estate is in effect (if the spouse did not make the election), 
expenses related to the property (taxes, upkeep, etc.) are allocated between 
the spouse (life tenant) and the descendants (remaindermen) under the 
rules of another chapter (chapter 738).   
○​ If the spouse elects the one-half interest option, expenses are then 
allocated proportionally between the spouse and descendants as tenants in 
common.   
4.​ Exceptions & special circumstances 
○​ This statute does not apply if the property was owned by the decedent as a 
tenancy by the entireties (married couple) or joint tenancy with right of 
survivorship.   
○​ If the surviving spouse disclaims the life estate (under chapter 739), the 
descendants’ interests may not be divested.   
Under Florida Statute 732.401, the surviving spouse’s life estate vests automatically at 
the moment of death if: 
●​ The property qualifies as protected homestead 
●​ The decedent is survived by a spouse and one or more descendants 
●​ There was no valid devise
