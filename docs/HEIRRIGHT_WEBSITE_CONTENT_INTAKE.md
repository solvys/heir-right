Source posture: combines the live public site at [heirright.com](https://heirright.com/), the onboarding questionnaire, the Zoom-note synthesis, and the workflow packet planning docs.

## What The Public Site Is Saying Today

Current homepage themes observed on June 3, 2026:

- HeirRight presents itself as a family-owned inherited-property solution serving South Florida and Texas.
- The primary promise is fast relief: attorney fees covered, liens/tax issues handled, co-heir disputes handled, and as-is purchases.
- The hero CTA is a free consultation / cash-offer form tied to inherited-property liquidation.
- Trust proof currently leans on testimonials, transaction speed, Joshua's founder story, and a white-glove tone.
- The visual voice is broad cash-homebuyer language with probate/inheritance positioning layered in.

## Positioning Direction For The Redesign

Keep:

- Inherited-property specialization.
- Plainspoken promise that the team handles the hard parts.
- Human guidance during a stressful estate situation.
- South Florida credibility and Joshua-led trust language.

Sharpen:

- Lead with "inheritance recovery / estate settlement" instead of generic cash-buyer framing.
- Explain the real operator steps: review title, taxes, heirs, probate friction, and next-action clarity.
- Show that complicated title and family situations are handled carefully, not just quickly.
- Separate "free review" language from any claim that an offer is automatic.

Avoid:

- Over-promising legal outcomes.
- Talking like a generic wholesaler.
- Heavy engineering language from the product repo.
- Implying live outreach, instant qualification, or guaranteed results.

## Recommended Site Structure

1. Hero
   One clear inherited-property headline, subhead, trust proof, and primary consultation CTA.
2. How HeirRight Helps
   Explain estate settlement friction in plain language: title, taxes, liens, missing heirs, probate.
3. Process
   Show the guided sequence: review the property, identify issues, map the heirs, outline options, close on the timeline that fits.
4. Proof
   Testimonials, founder credibility, region served, and "what we handle."
5. Fit Check
   Situations served: inherited house, title problems, tax issues, vacant/problem property, multiple heirs.
6. Contact / Intake
   Simple request form and direct call path.

## Direction Constraints For S10-T2

- Mobile-first viewport must read as trust-building, not hype-heavy.
- Designs should feel premium and human, not like a generic investor landing page.
- Trust treatment should include process clarity, not just badges and reviews.
- The first screen should make the next step obvious: request a review, schedule a call, or see how the process works.
- Prototype copy should stay within approved themes already visible on the live site and repo planning docs.
